The problem

Dependability, not effect size

  • A large effect from one guide, one donor, or a pseudobulk of a handful of cells can be an artifact.
  • In target prioritization, every false lead spends a validation experiment on the wrong target.
  • So dependability matters as much as magnitude.

The method

A measurement, decomposed

Each perturbation effect is a measurement in a crossed design. Two facets are random, meaning we generalize across them; one is fixed, meaning we want consistency within its levels.

Target Guide · random Donor · random Condition · fixed
Rdep,t= 1 1 + 1 − RguideRguide + 1 − RdonorRdonor
St=Mt×Rdep,t

Rdep,t is the correlation expected between two independent repeats of the experiment, run with fresh guides and fresh donors.

Research question 1 · the ranking

Does ranking by dependability reorder the shortlist that effect size alone produces?

50 / 100
top effect-only hits drop out of the dependability top-100
Spearman ρ = 0.74 overall · RPS3 #79 → #4 · 7,258 scored

Yes. Weighting effect size by dependability moves reproducible targets up and strong but fragile ones down.

Research question 2 · context

Within an activation state, does dependability separate a context-specific signal from noise?

Yes. With no gene labels, the method reconstructs the T-cell-receptor module. The CD3 complex, ZAP70 and LAT are reliably measurable only in activated T cells.

92 targets flagged context-specific but reliable.
Resting dependability sits at the noise floor.

Research question 3 · the design

How dependable is the screen as a whole?

Eρ² ≈ .44

The panel's reliability: its correlation with an independent replication. Adequate to rank targets, short of the .70 an absolute threshold would need.

Research question 4 · improving the design

Which facet limits dependability, and how much would a future screen recover?

Two guides cap generalizability near 0.53.
No number of donors reaches 0.70; about 15 guides do.

Guides, not donors. Adding guides raises dependability; adding donors barely moves it, and this holds within every activation state.

Conclusion

Dependability, not effect size, is what a reproducible screen should rank on.

Generalizability theory gives each target a dependability coefficient that effect size cannot supply. On this screen it reorders the validation shortlist, reconstructs the TCR module from reproducibility alone, and shows the design is limited by guides rather than donors. The full pipeline is described in the paper; every number regenerates from one re-run.
