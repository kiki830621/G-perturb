G-perturb

A perturbation hit seen in one guide, or one donor, might be measurement noise. G-perturb ranks CD4+ T-cell Perturb-seq drug targets by whether the readout is dependable — not by how large it is.

The problem

Dependability, not effect size

Genome-wide Perturb-seq screens rank candidate targets by differential-expression strength. That skips a measurement question: is the readout dependable? A large effect measured in a single guide, a single donor, or a pseudobulk of a handful of cells can be an artifact — and for target prioritization, where every false lead costs a validation experiment, dependability matters as much as magnitude.

The method

A measurement, decomposed

We treat each perturbation's transcriptional effect as a measurement in a crossed design and apply generalizability theory (Cronbach). Two facets are random — you want to generalize across them. One is fixed — you want consistency, not sampling generalization.

Target Guide · random Donor · random Condition · fixed

The ranking scalar is a per-target dependability, R_dep,t — the joint generalizability over the random facets, error-to-signal ratios adding. It is exactly the correlation you would see between two independent redos of the whole experiment, fresh guides and fresh donors:

R_dep,t = 1 / ( 1 + (1−R^guide)/R^guide + (1−R^donor)/R^donor )
// score S_t = E_t × R_dep,t // effect magnitude × dependability

The estimator is a precision-weighted crossed mixed model (REML), fit per gene on the real 44.6 GB joint pseudobulk — the selected method, chosen by pre-registered synthetic recovery.

Finding · measurement error

The signal was hidden under noise

Pseudobulk residual is inflated by measurement error — many cells aggregate few single cells. The non-targeting controls give a direct per-gene estimate of that noise floor. Remove it, and the dependable hits that were there all along come back.

Finding · the bottleneck

Reliability is guide-limited

A D-study projects dependability onto alternative designs. The guide error term dominates the donor term; with only two guides per gene the coefficient caps near 0.53, and no number of donors reaches 0.70. To improve a screen, add guides, not people — and this holds within every activation state, not just pooled.

Finding · the ranking

The list reshuffles

Rank each target by effect × dependability, combining guide and donor reproducibility the right way — errors adding, never a product of coefficients. Strong-but-unreliable hits fall; reproducible ones rise. The reshuffle is the deliverable.

Finding · context — the sharpest test

Reliable only when the T cell is activated

Because condition is a fixed facet — Rest, 8 h, 48 h are chosen states — the textbook treatment is a separate analysis within each. Slicing the dependability by activation state surfaces targets dependable in one state but not another. With no gene labels, the method reconstructs the T-cell-receptor signaling module.

The CD3 complex, ZAP70 and LAT surface as reliably measurable only in activated T cells — resting dependability sits at the noise floor. Knock out the receptor machinery, and the effect only appears once the receptor is switched on.
